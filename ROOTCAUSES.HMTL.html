Focus Question: Why isnt student-teacher review more widely instituted?

Student-Teacher review has been accredited to give accurate results and yet it is not commonly insistuted in spaces of public education.

Root Cause #1: We have alternative systems of review
Evidence 1: The Teaching Performance Assesment "This assessment of teaching performance is designed to measure the candidate's knowledge, skills and ability with relation to California's Teaching Performance Expectations (TPEs)"
Reasoning 1: Assesments do not take into account the emotional aspect of teaching. The criteria for a teacher to get their initial credentials are impersonal and barely effective.
Evidence 2: An Academic Article on the best ways of teacher evaluations such as Value Added-Models, Classroom Obsverations, Prinicle Evaluations, and more reveals a shared problem that they cannot fully asses the classroom.
Reasoning 2: These test cannot accuratly describe the climate of a classroom, the test may be blind to underlying dynamics.
Implications for effective “solutions”: Instituting a review system by students who interact and learn from their teachers everyday
Root Cause #2: Districts are reluctant to fire their teachers.
Evidence 1: We have teacher shortages. "The U.S. has experienced recurring teacher shortages for decades—a condition the COVID-19 pandemic has only worsened."
Reasoning 1: Since teacher turnover rates are increasing year to year schools are reluctant to let teachers go.
Evidence 2: Unions make bad teachers hard to fire "Very rarely will teachers with tenure be dismissed over poor performance in the classroom. Plus, the process to fire an ineffective tenured teacher remains difficult and complicated."
Reasoning 2: The firing process is a long and hard one, making principle reluctant to listen to concerns.
Implications for effective “solutions”: If true teacher review was more institutionalized the teacher pool would be more qualified yet smaller. But if we truly cared about children's educations we would solve this problem by raising wages for teachers.
